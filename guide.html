EU Energy Label

The concept of energy label was first introduced in 2009 and has since then gained acceptance. The European Energy label scale ranges from A to G where A is the most efficient and G is the least efficient. The Energy label has helped consumers (users, dealers and distributors) to invest on products which are more energy efficient and also encourages manufacturer to produce innovative technologies which are energy efficient. The running cost of an appliance can be compared with other similar products by comparing their labels. About two years ago, the energy label has been widely recognized by 93% consumers and more than half of consumer consider it when purchasing energy efficient appliances according to the Special Eurobarometer 492. It is the desire of all manufacturer to see their energy labelled product ranked highest in category when compared with other competitors. To this effect, manufacturer who sell appliances that is less efficient are aiming to improve the performance of their product to the highest category.
